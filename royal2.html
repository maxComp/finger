The Royal Restuarant Company 836 Main
Idaho Statesman April 2, 1896


Notice is hereby given that the Royal Restaurant Company have bought the Union restaurant business at No. 836 Main Street, in the city of Boise, and said Royal Restaurant Company will not be responsible for any debt of the former. All bills due must be presented at once or not later than April 3, 1896. The Royal Restaurant Company will conduct a first class restaurant in every respect

NEW BUILDINGS BREAK ALL RECORDS
Idaho Statesman October 10, 1910


Nearly Six Hundred Thousand Dollars Represented in Those Now Going Up. Latest Ideas in Modern Construction Form Conspicuous Feature of Work. Among the many smaller business buildings under construction at this time are the Larson building between Eleventh and Twelfth on Main. Which is a two-story addition to the present Larson building and will be occupied as a rooming house. Its estimated cost is $20,000 and it will be completed by the 1st of November.

Restaurant-Bar Opens
Idaho Statesman October 5, 1947


A combination restaurant and bar opened Oct. 1 at 1112. Main street and is operated by Vince Aguirre and Ed Graves, Named the New Royal, the establishment appears to be the most recent word in modern decor. Twenty-seven red leather upholstered stools line a bar finished in birch, prima vera and mahogany and faces eight overstuffed booths done in red and gray leather. The entire motif follows a red and gray color scheme. A "different" type wall finish has been applied along two of the walls; finished in a flat gray paint, striking gold spots form an attractive pattern. Large photographs of Idaho scenes are paneled behind the back bar and ample mirroring fills in along that wall. At present, the Royal plans to specialize in businessmen's lunches and will concentrate on food at reasonable prices, Aguirre stated. He added that the bar sells only beer now, although a liquor license may be applied for at a later date.
